Technical Challenge: AI & Process Automation Specialist

Role: AI & Process Automation Specialist - Process Architecture

Time: ~4–6 hours over up to 2 days. Ship what you can, we'd rather see a rough, working

end-to-end tool than a half-built polished one.

What to submit: A GitHub repo (public or share access) & a video or documented

walkthrough.



The scenario

You've just embedded with our Disputes team for a morning. Here's the workflow.



When an issuing bank raises a chargeback against one of our merchants, the case lands in an analyst's queue. The analyst has to decide whether to represent the chargeback - i.e. push back against the issuer with compelling evidence - or accept the liability and let the merchant take the loss.

Each case in the queue contains:





The chargeback reason code raised by the issuer (e.g. Visa 13.1 "Merchandise/Services Not Received", Mastercard 4837 "No Cardholder Authorisation", and ~13 others in common rotation)



Transaction metadata (merchant, MCC, amount, currency, AVS/CVV result, 3DS status, IP, device fingerprint, timestamps, BIN country)



A short issuer narrative - the reason text the issuing bank submitted on behalf of the cardholder



0-4 merchant evidence documents uploaded in response to our representment request - typically PDFs and images (delivery confirmations, AVS match logs, terms of service screenshots, prior transaction history with the same card, comms between merchant and cardholder)

For each case, the analyst currently:





Looks up the compelling evidence requirements for that specific reason code under that specific scheme. Visa and Mastercard have different rules, and every reason code has its own checklist.



Goes through the merchant's uploaded evidence and decides whether it actually satisfies that checklist - or whether the merchant has uploaded something tangentially related but useless (this happens a lot).



Writes a 3-5 sentence representment rationale for the file.



Flags a recommended action: represent (we have a defensible case), accept_liability (we don't), or request_more_evidence (the merchant's submission has a fixable gap).

A senior analyst told you: "The decision itself is usually clear in 90 seconds. The other 18 minutes is me re-reading the scheme rules, opening four PDFs to check one date, and writing up why. If a tool could just lay it all out for me — here's the rule, here's the evidence, here's the gap — I'd get through twice as many cases.



Your task

Build a tool that takes a chargeback case as input and produces an analyst-ready representment workup.



Required functionality

Your tool must:





Accept a case as input. We've provided a small synthetic dataset (see "Provided materials" below) - 10 cases as JSON, each with reason code, transaction metadata, issuer narrative, and references to 0–4 merchant evidence files (mix of PDFs and images, also provided).



Use an LLM to produce a structured representment workup containing:





Reason code summary - a plain-English restatement of what the issuer is alleging and what compelling evidence the scheme requires to defend it



Evidence assessment - for each compelling-evidence requirement, does the merchant's submission satisfy it? (satisfied / partial / missing), with a pointer to which document and where



Representment rationale (3-5 sentences) ready for the analyst to edit and file Recommended action (represent / accept_liability / request_more_evidence) with a one-line justification



If request_more_evidence: a specific list of what to ask the merchant for



Handle the documents. Merchant evidence is in PDFs and images - delivery proofs, AVS logs, screenshots of T&Cs, etc. Your call how to process them: OCR, vision models, text extraction, or explicitly scoping out images with a note on the limitation. We want to see how you reason about the tradeoff.



Expose it to an analyst. A minimal UI is fine - Gradio, Streamlit, a Retool-style mockup, even a well-designed CLI. The point is: imagine an analyst working 80 cases a day. What do they need to see at a glance, and what do they need to be able to override?



Be runnable. A README that lets us run it end-to-end in under 10 minutes from git clone. Include a .env.example. Use whatever LLM provider you like (Anthropic, OpenAI, local). 



What we're explicitly NOT asking for





Production-grade error handling, auth, or deployment



A custom-trained model - use a frontier LLM API



Real scheme rules in their entirety - we'll give you a simplified version of the compelling evidence requirements for the reason codes in the dataset. Use those.



Beautiful design (a clean, functional UI beats a pretty broken one)



Coverage of edge cases beyond what's in the 10 provided cases



Provided materials

You'll receive a zip with:





cases.json - 10 synthetic chargeback cases with reason code, transaction metadata, issuer narrative, and document references



documents/ - ~25 PDF and image files referenced by the cases (synthetic delivery confirmations, AVS logs, T&Cs screenshots, etc.) 



reason_codes.md - the 15 reason codes in the dataset with simplified compelling-evidence requirements per scheme



README.md - schema details



A few cases are deliberately ambiguous: the merchant has uploaded evidence that looks relevant but doesn't actually meet the requirement, or the requirement is met but buried inside a 12-page PDF. That's intentional. How your tool surfaces uncertainty and points the analyst at the right evidence matters more than whether it gets every recommendation "right."
